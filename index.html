About

A Journey Shaped by Faith, Mission, and Scholarship

Rev. Dr. Vinod S. Sailus serves as Professor of Missiology at St. Peter’s Pontifical Institute, Bengaluru. His work integrates pastoral ministry, theological scholarship, and intercultural dialogue, contributing to contemporary missiology and Indian Christian theology.

Timeline

1980 — Born in Kerala, India
2004–2008 — BD, Gurukul Lutheran Theological College
2009 — Ordained Presbyter, Church of South India
2013–2015 — M.Th. in Missiology, UTC Bengaluru
2016–2019 — Doctoral Studies (D.Th.), UTC Bengaluru
2021–2025 — Associate Professor & Head, Gurukul Lutheran Theological College
2025–Present — Professor of Missiology, St. Peter’s Pontifical Institute

Publications

  • Borderless God (2018)
  • Borderless Spirituality (2023)
  • The Development of Missiological Thinking and Praxis (2025)
  • The Dynamics of Mission in Transition (2025)
  • Doing Mission with Muslims (2026)

Teaching

Teaching focuses on intercultural theology, mission studies, contextual Christian witness, and interfaith dialogue, including supervision of BD, M.Th., D.Min., and Ph.D. scholars.

Mission

Over three decades of ministerial experience including missionary service in Telangana, leadership in CSI mission schools, and pastoral ministry as an ordained Presbyter.
